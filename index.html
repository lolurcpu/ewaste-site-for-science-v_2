Recycle Old Devices Responsibly

Protecting our health and environment through smart e-waste recycling.

What is E-Waste?

Electronic waste, or e-waste, includes discarded electrical or electronic devices such as computers, mobile phones, TVs, and kitchen appliances. E-waste is the fastest-growing waste stream in the world due to rapid technological advancements and consumer demand.

Environmental Hazards of E-Waste

According to the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), e-waste contains harmful substances such as lead, mercury, cadmium, and flame retardants. If improperly handled, these toxins can contaminate soil, groundwater, and air, posing serious environmental and health risks.

Learn more: EPA on Electronics Waste

Health Impacts from Informal Recycling

Informal recycling in developing countries often involves unsafe practices like open-air burning and acid baths. These processes release toxic chemicals into the environment, exposing workers and nearby residents to severe health issues. Research published in PubMed highlights increased levels of DNA damage, respiratory illnesses, and neurotoxicity among workers exposed to e-waste.

Current Recycling Rates and the Global Crisis

Despite global awareness, only about 20% of e-waste is properly recycled. The rest is dumped in landfills or processed through unsafe, informal methods.

According to the United Nations University (UNU), over 53.6 million metric tonnes of e-waste were generated globally in 2019, and that number is expected to exceed 74 million tonnes by 2030.

Source: UNU Global E-Waste Report

What You Can Do

  • Find Certified Recyclers: Use recycling centers certified by R2 or e-Stewards to ensure environmentally responsible processing.
  • Participate in Take-Back Programs: Brands like Apple, Dell, and Best Buy offer programs to return used electronics for recycling.
  • Donate Working Devices: Many charities and schools accept functional devices for reuse, reducing demand for new production.
  • Spread Awareness: Educate friends and family about the dangers of improper disposal and the benefits of responsible recycling.
  • Check Local Laws: Some states and cities offer special e-waste collection days or have regulations for disposal.
